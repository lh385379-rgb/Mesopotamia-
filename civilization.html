Mesopotamia was located in the Fertile Crescent, a region with rich soil that allowed early farming to thrive. The availability of water from the Tigris and Euphrates Rivers helped cities grow.

Early Mesopotamian cities such as Ur, Uruk, and Babylon were independent city-states. Each city-state had its own government, laws, and ruler. Kings were responsible for protection, law enforcement, and maintaining order.
