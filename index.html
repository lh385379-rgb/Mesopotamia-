Mesopotamia: The Cradle of Civilization

Mesopotamia was one of the world’s first civilizations and is often called the “Cradle of Civilization.” It developed around 3500 BCE in the region between the Tigris and Euphrates Rivers in the Middle East.

Many important ideas that shape modern life began in Mesopotamia, including writing, laws, organized government, and advanced farming.
